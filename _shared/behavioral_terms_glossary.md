# Behavioral Terms Glossary

- Choice architecture: The design of environments in which people encounter choices.
- Nudge: A change in choice presentation that influences behavior while preserving formal choice.
- Default: A preselected or institutionally favored option.
- Friction: Effort, complexity, delay, or hassle that reduces action.
- Salience: Visibility or attention-grabbing quality.
- Administrative burden: Learning, compliance, and psychological costs created by institutions.
- Dark pattern: Interface design that manipulates or coerces users against their interests.
- Boost: An intervention designed to strengthen decision capacity rather than steer behavior.
